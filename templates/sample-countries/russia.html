Russia

Introduction

Russia, the largest country in the world by land area, spans across Eastern Europe and northern Asia with a population of approximately 143.4 million people. It is classified as a More Economically Developed Country (MEDC) with a Human Development Index (HDI) of 0.824, reflecting moderate to high levels of income, health, and education.

Covering a vast geographical area of 17,098,242 square kilometers, Russia is known for its diverse landscapes, from tundra and forests to mountains and lakes.

The country is currently led by President Vladimir Putin, who holds a far-right political stance, emphasizing strong central authority, national sovereignty, and economic reform.

Russia’s significant natural resources, military power, and geopolitical influence make it a major global player.

Russia's Investment Risk Analysis

A credit rating measures the ability of a country or entity to repay debt, assigned by agencies like Moody’s, S&P, or Fitch. Ratings range from high-grade investment ratings (e.g., AAA, A1) indicating low risk, to speculative or junk ratings (e.g., B, C) signaling high risk and potential default. A1 is a high-grade rating, reflecting strong financial stability, but external factors like economic shifts or geopolitical risks can still impact performance.

The equity risk premium (ERP) is the extra return investors expect from stocks compared to risk-free assets like government bonds. For example, an ERP of 5.63% suggests equities are perceived as moderately risky, offering higher potential returns to compensate for volatility and uncertainty. A higher ERP indicates greater risk, while a lower ERP reflects market confidence.
