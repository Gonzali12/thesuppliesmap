United States of America

Introduction

The United States of America, located in North America, has a population of approximately 331.89 million people. It is classified as a More Economically Developed Country (MEDC) with a Human Development Index (HDI) of 0.926, highlighting its high standards of living, education, and healthcare.

Covering a vast geographical area of 9,833,520 square kilometers, the USA is known for its diverse landscapes, economic power, and cultural influence.

The country is currently led by President Joe Biden, who holds a center-left political stance, focusing on economic recovery, social equity, and international cooperation.

The USA’s strong economy, technological advancements, and global leadership position make it a major world power.

USA's Investment Risk Analysis

A credit rating measures the ability of a country or entity to repay debt, assigned by agencies like Moody’s, S&P, or Fitch. Ratings range from high-grade investment ratings (e.g., AAA, A1) indicating low risk, to speculative or junk ratings (e.g., B, C) signaling high risk and potential default. A1 is a high-grade rating, reflecting strong financial stability, but external factors like economic shifts or geopolitical risks can still impact performance.

The equity risk premium (ERP) is the extra return investors expect from stocks compared to risk-free assets like government bonds. For example, an ERP of 5.63% suggests equities are perceived as moderately risky, offering higher potential returns to compensate for volatility and uncertainty. A higher ERP indicates greater risk, while a lower ERP reflects market confidence.
