Philippines

Introduction

The Philippines, an archipelagic country in Southeast Asia, has a population of approximately 113.12 million people. It is classified as a More Economically Developed Country (MEDC) with a Human Development Index (HDI) of 0.718, reflecting moderate levels of income, health, and education.

Covering a geographical area of 300,000 square kilometers, the Philippines is known for its thousands of islands, tropical climate, and rich biodiversity.

The country is currently led by President Ferdinand Marcos Jr., who holds a center-right political stance, focusing on economic growth, infrastructure development, and social welfare.

The Philippines' strategic location, cultural diversity, and natural resources play crucial roles in its economy and regional influence.
