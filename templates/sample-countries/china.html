China

Introduction

China, officially the People's Republic of China, is a major country in East Asia with an HDI of 0.768, indicating high human development. It is classified as a More Economically Developed Country (MEDC) due to its significant economic growth and global influence.

Covering approximately 9.6 million square kilometers, China is the fourth-largest country by land area.

Xi Jinping currently serves as the President. With a population exceeding 1.4 billion, China is the most populous country globally.

Rich in natural resources such as coal, iron ore, petroleum, and natural gas, China is also a leading producer of agricultural products like rice and wheat, underpinning its economic stability and global standing.

The equity risk premium (ERP) is the extra return investors expect from stocks compared to risk-free assets like government bonds. For example, an ERP of 5.63% suggests equities are perceived as moderately risky, offering higher potential returns to compensate for volatility and uncertainty. A higher ERP indicates greater risk, while a lower ERP reflects market confidence.
